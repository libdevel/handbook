Publish a Guide

Guides may be set to Published, Unpublished, Private, or Archived as HTML.

Status Types

Published

All published guides must offer the best possible user experience, presenting a strong professional ethos consistent with the Library's other brand and identity standards. Because published guides are listed categorically on the Guides home page, and are discoverable by search, all published guides are subject to the style and content guidelines outlined in this handbook.

Private

While private guides are not listed categorically on the Guides home page, they are still accessible to patrons who have the URL. Thus, like any publicly accessible guides, private guides too must offer the best possible user experience and conform to brand and identity standards. The exception is Internal guides, which may adopt any page structure or information architectue, although we recommend the Internal Guide (1 column) template.

Unpublished

Guide authors should unpublish guides which are no longer current and/or which are undergoing significant revision. Unpublished guides are not accessible to patrons, even those who know the URL, and are not listed categorically on the Guides home page.

Archived

Guides which are no longer needed should be deleted from the LibGuides platform, where possible. However, to preserve content that may be wanted later, there are two ways in which to archive a guide:

Soft Archiving
By simply unpublishing a guide, you are both archiving the guide and preserving the guide for republication in future.
Hard Archiving
For those who like to clean house, you may create an HTML backup and delete the guide from the LibGuides platform. This will preserve as a Web page the essential structure and content of the guide, including links.

The HTML backup cannot be re-imported as a functioning guide. It is useful only as a record of content/structure.
