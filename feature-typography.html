Text (Typography)

Organize your content with strong typographic hierarchy. Foreground certain content with powerful styles.

Headings

Use headings to subdivide the content in your Rich Text / HTML boxes.

Limited Headings
For accessibility reasons, headings H1 and H2 are not available in the WYSIWYG editor and should not be used in Rich Text / HTML boxes.

This is an H3 heading. It's the largest header.

<h3>This is an H3 heading. It's the largest header.</h3>

This is an H4 heading. It has a bottom border. 

<h4>This is an H4 heading. It has a bottom border.</h4>
This is an H5 heading. It's a bolded sub-header.
<h5>This is an H5 heading. It's a bolded sub-header.</h5>
This is an H6 heading. It's an italicized sub-sub-header.
<h6>This is an H6 heading. It's an italicized sub-sub-header.</h6>

Body Text

Standardized Fonts
Except in special cases, such as foreign languages or maths symbols, please do not change font face or font size in your guides.

Paragraphs make up the bulk of the text you will create.

They are left-aligned and vertically block-spaced.

<p>Paragraphs make up the bulk of the text you will create.</p>
<p>The are left-aligned and vertically double-spaced.</p>

Lead Text

Lead text may be used sparingly at the beginning of larger content blocks (like pages) to summarize content or focus readers.

<p class="lead">Lead text may be used sparingly at the beginning of larger
content blocks (like pages) to summarize content or focus readers.</p>

Blockquotes

Use blockquotes as epigraphs for content blocks (or pages).

Someone famous in Source Title
<blockquote>
  <p>Use blockquotes as epigraphs for content blocks (or pages).</p>
  <footer>
     Someone famous in <cite title="Source Title">Source Title</cite>
  </footer>
</blockquote>

Notes and Alerts

You may also wish to prepend your alert messages with special icons.

Use this container to sound a note of affirmation or success.

<div class="alert alert-success" role="alert">
<p>
<i class="fa fa-star fa-spacer-right" aria-hidden="true"></i>
Use this container to sound a note of affirmation or success.
</p>
</div>

Use this container to sound a purely informational note.

<div class="alert alert-info" role="alert">
<p>
<i class="fa fa-info-circle fa-spacer-right" aria-hidden="true"></i>
Use this container to sound a purely informational note.
</p>
</div>

Use this container to sound a note of caution.

<div class="alert alert-warning" role="alert">
<p>
<i class="fa fa-star fa-exclamation-triangle" aria-hidden="true"></i>
Use this container to sound a note of caution.
</p>
</div>

Use this container to sound a note of severe warning or to indicate prohibition of some action.

<div class="alert alert-danger" role="alert">
<p>
<i class="fa fa-star fa-times-circle" aria-hidden="true"></i>
Use this container to sound a note of severe warning or to indicate prohibition of some action.
</p>
</div>

Use this special container for subtle introductory/explanatory descriptions.

<div class="alert alert-intro" role="alert">
<p>
Use this special container for subtle introductory/explanatory descriptions.
</p>
</div>

Special Text Formats

Computer Code
Math Variables a + b = c 
<var>a</var> + <var>b</var> = <var>c</var>
Keyboard Keys CTRL + Z 
<kbd>CTRL</kbd> + <kbd>Z</kbd>
Computer Code <p>This is a paragraph element</p> 
<code><p>This is a paragraph element</p></code>
Sample Computer Output You have pressed a key! 
<samp>You have pressed a key!</samp>
Styled Text
Marker Text Pay attention to this 
Pay attention <mark>to this</mark>
Typewriter Typewriter Text 
<tt>Typewriter Text</tt>
Linethrough This and not that 
This and <del>not that</del>
Small Text Though smaller (still important!) 
Though smaller <small>(still important!)</small>
Big Text I wish...I were big! 
I wish...<big>I were big!</big>
Superscript Stuart Hall PhD 
Stuart Hall <sup>PhD</sup>
Subscript Water, a.k.a. H2O 
Water, a.k.a. H<sub>2</sub>O
Uppercase

make this uppercase!

 
<p class="text-uppercase">make this uppercase!</p>
