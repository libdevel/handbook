Guide Requirements

There are two distinct variants of Subject Guides. No distinction is made between them for users of the LibGuides platform. However, each uses a separate template and has different requirements for guide creators.

Disciplinary Subject Guides (was DDW)

Disciplinary Subject Guides must be structured to embody the shape of research practice in a particular field. They must move from the general to the specific, first offering an introduction to the subject, followed by groupings of tertiary, secondary, and primary resources.

List of Disciplinary Subjects

Requirements At-a-glance
1 Are based on the Subject Guide template, but are created by copying the extant guide:
“ * Disciplinary Subject Template ”
2 Are associated with a Subject Librarian, who remains the luminous point of contact for that subject.
2 Are associated with a Library Website Pathfinder page.

Standard Subject Guides

Standard Subject Guides are more flexibly structured, as they focus around subject areas far more specific than an entire discipline. They also should move from the general to the specific, although the information architecture will vary according to the use case(s) defined by the author.

Requirements At-a-glance
1 Are based on the Subject Guide template.
2 Are associated with a Subject Librarian, who remains the luminous point of contact for that subject.
2 Are not associated directly with a Library Website Pathfinder page, but should be linked to from the Related Guides box in the associated Disciplinary Subject guide.
