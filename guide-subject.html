Guide Requirements

There are two distinct variants of Subject Guides. No distinction is made between them for users of the LibGuides platform. However, each uses a separate template and has different requirements for guide creators.

General Subject Guides (formerly DDW)

General Subject Guides correspond to disciplinary offerings at Notre Dame. General Subject Guides should aim to move from the general to the specific, grouping resources by tertiary, secondary, and primary resources. Variants of the information architecture represented by the General Subject Template are possible, with approval by the LMT, as long as they are structured to embody the shape of research practice in their particular field.

Specific Subject Guides

Standard Subject Guides are more flexibly structured, as they focus around topics within a particular subject area. They also should move from the general to the specific, although the information architecture will vary according to the use case defined by the author.

Requirements At-a-glance
1 Are based on the Subject Guide template.
2 Far greater freedom to structure its information architecture

Introductory Notes

Introductory notes are meant to help those new to academia understand the role of the resource gathered under that box in academic research.

Dictionaries are most often collections of words in one or more specific languages, usually arranged in alphabetical order, providing information like word meanings, usage, etymology, and, when they contain more than one language, translations. Some dictionaries include information more commonly found in encyclopedias.
