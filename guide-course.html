Guide Requirements

Course guides should be organized so that they help students to use the Library and information resources in ways that promote their success in a particular course. Librarians work closely with instructors to decide the best resources and organization for a course guide. However, Librarians are responsible for designing, building, and maintaining that course guide, as instructors are not eligible for LibGuides accounts.

The Right Tool
Help your instructor understand the proper scope and role of the course guide. Things like assignments and grading rubrics are best embedded in a learning management platform such as Sakai.

Requirements At-a-glance
1 Guide navigation based on the Course Guide template (left navigation; single right content column).
2 Guide title must follow course title conventions.
3 Should provide a Course Overview box to introduce the course description and instructor.
4 Only the most recent version of any instructor's guide may be published. Versions from previous semesters/years must be unpublished.
5 Resources should complement rather than compete with any LMS or portfolio software.

Title Formatter

Course titles must conform to the approved format. Because course guide titles tend to be more complex, we recommend using the Title Formatter.

Librarians should not add their names to course guide titles. Students searching for a specific librarian's guides, may search for all guides by a specific librarian.

AFST 24100 — Introduction to Africana Studies: The Problematic of Race in America (Mohammed)

SOC 13181 / POLS 13181 — Social Science University Seminar: Immigration and Citizenship (Johnson)



                     
 

                  


               

               


               
Course Overview Box

               
Unless your instructor wants a Jumbotron to open their course guide, the first box of the first page of the course guide should contain an overview of the course. You should Include a course description, instructor's contact information, and a link to the Sakai course page. Optionally, include an instructor's photo.
